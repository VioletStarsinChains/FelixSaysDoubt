PLAINTIFF’S REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION OF DOCUMENTS

⸻

Request No. 4 – CT Imaging Data
Produce the complete and unredacted imaging records of Plaintiff’s temporal bone CT scan(s) performed at University of Minnesota / M Health Fairview for the evaluation of superior canal dehiscence syndrome (SCDS) or related vestibular conditions. This request encompasses:
	1.	All raw imaging files in DICOM format, including axial, coronal, sagittal, and oblique slices, as well as any 3D reconstructions generated by the scanner software.
	2.	Radiologist notes, measurement logs, and annotations related to the scan, including any preliminary or draft interpretations not provided to Plaintiff.
	3.	Technologist logs documenting scan settings, slice thickness, resolution, and any protocol adjustments made during Plaintiff’s CT.
	4.	Internal communications among radiologists, neurologists, otolaryngologists, or Fairview staff regarding Plaintiff’s CT results, particularly concerning evidence of superior canal dehiscence or differential diagnoses.
	5.	All versions of radiology reports transmitted to Plaintiff or providers, including preliminary, amended, or addendum reports.
	6.	Any communications with external consultants or researchers regarding Plaintiff’s CT images or findings.
	7.	Policies or protocols governing the disclosure of DICOM files to patients, and any exceptions applied in Plaintiff’s case.

⸻

Request No. 5 – Data Retention and Sharing
Produce all records reflecting whether Plaintiff’s CT scan data has been shared, uploaded, or transmitted to third-party repositories, research databases, contractors, or federal agencies.

⸻

PURPOSE

These requests are intended to ensure Plaintiff has access to the complete imaging record necessary to independently evaluate the presence or absence of superior canal dehiscence, and to determine whether University of Minnesota / M Health Fairview withheld, altered, or dual-purposed diagnostic information.
