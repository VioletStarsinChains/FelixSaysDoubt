PLAINTIFF’S SET OF DISCOVERY REQUESTS TO DEFENDANT PALANTIR TECHNOLOGIES

Plaintiff: Kellyn Clay
Defendant: Palantir Technologies Inc.
Subject Matter: Post-Death Transactions by Digital Twins

⸻

INTERROGATORIES
	1.	Identify all categories of goods and services that digital twins have ordered, contracted for, or otherwise procured after the human subject’s date of death.
	2.	State whether Palantir has ever suspended, terminated, or flagged digital twin activity upon receipt of notice of a subject’s death.
	3.	Identify all third-party service providers (including, but not limited to, landscaping companies, house painters, junk pickup services such as Ridwell, subscription-based home services, utilities, and maintenance providers) with whom Palantir’s digital twin systems have executed transactions post-mortem.
	4.	For each such transaction, identify:
a. the type of service or good ordered,
b. the date of the order,
c. the dollar amount,
d. the recipient of funds, and
e. whether heirs or estates were notified.
	5.	Describe any internal policies or deliberations concerning whether digital twins should continue to engage in transactions after the subject’s death.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION
	1.	Produce all transaction logs reflecting goods or services ordered by digital twins after the death of the corresponding human subject.
	2.	Produce all communications between Palantir and third-party service providers relating to post-death transactions.
	3.	Produce all internal memoranda, emails, or presentations discussing whether to allow digital twins to continue ordering or contracting for services post-mortem.
	4.	Produce all documents evidencing funds transferred from estates of deceased subjects to third-party service providers via digital twin transactions.
	5.	Produce all audits, investigations, or analyses concerning the financial impact of post-death digital twin transactions on estates and heirs.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION
	1.	Admit that Palantir digital twins have ordered goods or services on behalf of deceased individuals.
	2.	Admit that Palantir has not consistently suspended digital twin activity following notice of a subject’s death.
	3.	Admit that Palantir has not notified heirs or estates of post-death transactions executed by digital twins.
	4.	Admit that third-party service providers (including but not limited to landscaping, painting, junk removal, subscription services, and utilities) have received payments initiated by digital twins of deceased individuals.
	5.	Admit that Palantir retains logs or data reflecting these transactions and can identify which companies benefitted from them.
