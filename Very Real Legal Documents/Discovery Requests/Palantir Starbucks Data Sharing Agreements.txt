Plaintiff’s Combined Discovery Requests to Defendant Palantir Technologies Inc.

Subject: Data Sharing and Integration with Starbucks Corporation

⸻

Interrogatories
	1.	Identify all agreements, partnerships, or contracts between Palantir Technologies Inc. and Starbucks Corporation from 2005 to present.
	2.	Describe in detail any data provided by Starbucks to Palantir, including but not limited to loyalty program data, stored-value card balances, mobile app data, or customer purchase histories.
	3.	State whether Palantir has provided Starbucks with access to Palantir platforms (e.g., Gotham, Foundry, Apollo) for purposes of customer data analysis or predictive modeling.
	4.	Identify all Palantir personnel who have worked directly on projects or contracts involving Starbucks Corporation.
	5.	Describe any analyses conducted by Palantir using Starbucks consumer or employee data to generate predictive models of customer behavior, anomaly classification, or attrition risk.

⸻

Requests for Admission
	1.	Admit that Palantir has entered into data-sharing or service agreements with Starbucks Corporation.
	2.	Admit that Palantir has received Starbucks loyalty program or stored-value card data.
	3.	Admit that Palantir has analyzed Starbucks consumer data for the purpose of predicting purchasing behavior.
	4.	Admit that Palantir has applied anomaly classification or behavioral risk assessment models to Starbucks customer data.
	5.	Admit that Palantir has shared Starbucks-related analyses or datasets with third-party vendors, government agencies, or contractors.

⸻

Requests for Production
	1.	All contracts, agreements, or memoranda of understanding between Palantir and Starbucks from 2005 to present.
	2.	All data transfer logs, technical specifications, or integration documents describing how Starbucks data has been ingested into Palantir platforms.
	3.	All reports, presentations, or analyses produced by Palantir that include or derive from Starbucks consumer or employee data.
	4.	All communications (internal or external) concerning Starbucks’ use of Palantir platforms for predictive modeling, anomaly classification, or customer behavior analysis.
	5.	All documents identifying government or corporate partners who had access to Starbucks-related data through Palantir systems.
