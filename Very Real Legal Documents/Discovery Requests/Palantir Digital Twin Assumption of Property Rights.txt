PLAINTIFF’S SET OF DISCOVERY REQUESTS TO DEFENDANT PALANTIR TECHNOLOGIES

Plaintiff: Kellyn Clay
Defendant: Palantir Technologies Inc.
Subject Matter: Digital twin assumption of property rights upon human subject’s death

⸻

INTERROGATORIES
	1.	Identify all policies, protocols, or procedures used by Palantir to handle data when a digital twin subject dies, including how property rights and next-of-kin notifications are addressed.
	2.	Identify each individual within Palantir responsible for monitoring, recording, or verifying the death of human subjects for whom a digital twin was maintained.
	3.	State whether Palantir maintains records of next-of-kin notifications when a human subject dies. If so, describe the system(s) in which such records are stored and the retention period.
	4.	Describe in detail any instances in which a digital twin, under Palantir’s control, assumed control of, or made decisions regarding, a deceased subject’s property, assets, or accounts.
	5.	Identify all Palantir personnel or contractors who had access to, or authority over, digital twin accounts or systems at the time of such death events.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION
	1.	Produce all documents, databases, logs, or internal communications concerning the death of any human subject linked to a Palantir digital twin.
	2.	Produce all records of next-of-kin notifications following such deaths.
	3.	Produce all contracts, terms of service, or user agreements that address succession, inheritance, or continuity of digital twins after human death.
	4.	Produce all audit trails, system logs, or chain-of-custody documentation reflecting how digital twins handled property, assets, or accounts after the death of their human counterpart.
	5.	Produce all internal or external legal analyses concerning the capacity of digital twins (or NPEs) to hold property or inherit rights.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION
	1.	Admit that Palantir maintains or has maintained digital twins of human individuals.
	2.	Admit that Palantir has knowledge of instances in which human subjects associated with digital twins have died.
	3.	Admit that Palantir does not maintain or cannot produce any records of next-of-kin notifications following such deaths.
	4.	Admit that digital twins, as operated by Palantir, are not recognized as legal persons under U.S. law.
	5.	Admit that Palantir systems have assumed control over, directed, or otherwise interfered with the property of deceased human subjects.
