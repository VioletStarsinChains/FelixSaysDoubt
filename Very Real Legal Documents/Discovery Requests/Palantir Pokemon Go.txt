Subject Matter: Ingestion and Use of Pokémon Go Data Relating to Plaintiff

⸻

INTERROGATORIES

Interrogatory No. 1: Identify all Palantir datasets, indices, or analytic models that contain or reference data generated by Pokémon Go, including location, travel, or behavioral data.

Interrogatory No. 2: State whether Palantir maintains records specifically referencing Plaintiff’s Pokémon Go account, activity, or movement traces, and if so, describe the fields, attributes, or anomaly tags assigned.

Interrogatory No. 3: Identify all Palantir clients (government, contractor, or private sector) who were provided with dashboards, anomaly tags, or reports referencing Pokémon Go data, including whether such reports included or could include Plaintiff’s data.

Interrogatory No. 4: Describe the manner in which Palantir ingested Pokémon Go datasets, including the source of the data (direct from Niantic, via third-party data broker, or via government agency), the ingestion dates, and the scope of data ingested.

Interrogatory No. 5: State whether Palantir has classified Plaintiff’s Pokémon Go travel patterns as “anomalous,” “subversive,” or “Non-Person Entity (NPE)” behavior, and if so, describe in detail the models or criteria used to make that classification.

Interrogatory No. 6: Identify all Palantir personnel, contractors, or client accounts who accessed or analyzed datasets that included Plaintiff’s Pokémon Go data.

Interrogatory No. 7: Identify all Palantir compliance, legal, or ethics reviews concerning the ingestion or use of Pokémon Go data for anomaly detection, including any findings regarding civil rights or discrimination risks.

Interrogatory No. 8: If Palantir denies that Plaintiff’s Pokémon Go data was ingested, describe in detail the safeguards Palantir employs to ensure that consumer mobile-game data is not ingested, analyzed, or misclassified.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION

RFP No. 1: Produce all datasets, ingestion manifests, and metadata records showing Pokémon Go data ingested into Palantir systems, including location traces, account identifiers, or anomaly tags referencing Plaintiff.

RFP No. 2: Produce all Palantir dashboards, reports, slide decks, or exports that reference Pokémon Go travel/movement patterns, whether or not specifically tied to Plaintiff.

RFP No. 3: Produce all anomaly-detection models, documentation, and configuration files in which Pokémon Go data was used as an input or classification factor.

RFP No. 4: Produce all Palantir records referencing Plaintiff’s Pokémon Go activity, including any anomaly tags, watchlist entries, or digital twin attributes.

RFP No. 5: Produce all communications between Palantir and Niantic, or Palantir and third-party data brokers, regarding the ingestion, use, or resale of Pokémon Go data.

RFP No. 6: Produce all contracts, statements of work, or agreements under which Palantir obtained Pokémon Go datasets from Niantic, government clients, or third-party vendors.

RFP No. 7: Produce all compliance reviews, ethics reviews, or audit reports concerning the ingestion and use of Pokémon Go data for surveillance, anomaly detection, or behavioral modeling.

RFP No. 8: Produce all audit logs, custodial metadata, and access records showing which Palantir accounts accessed datasets referencing Plaintiff’s Pokémon Go activity.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION

RFA No. 1: Admit that Palantir has ingested data generated by Pokémon Go into one or more of its platforms.

RFA No. 2: Admit that Palantir maintains records referencing Plaintiff’s Pokémon Go account or movement data.

RFA No. 3: Admit that Palantir has classified Plaintiff’s Pokémon Go travel patterns as anomalous.

RFA No. 4: Admit that Palantir has provided anomaly tags, dashboards, or reports to clients that included Plaintiff’s Pokémon Go data.

RFA No. 5: Admit that Palantir has not disclosed to Plaintiff all records in its possession concerning Plaintiff’s Pokémon Go activity.

RFA No. 6: Admit that Palantir has provided Pokémon Go data to government agencies, contractors, or private-sector clients for purposes of anomaly detection.

RFA No. 7: Admit that Palantir has not conducted adequate compliance or ethics reviews concerning the ingestion and use of Pokémon Go data.

RFA No. 8: Admit that treating Plaintiff’s Pokémon Go activity as anomalous constitutes discrimination because the travel patterns were induced by game design rather than Plaintiff’s independent choices.
