PLAINTIFF’S COMBINED SET OF DISCOVERY REQUESTS TO DEFENDANT PALANTIR TECHNOLOGIES INC.

Plaintiff: Kellyn Clay
Defendant: Palantir Technologies Inc.
Subject Matter: Use of Housing/Employment Instability Patterns in Anomaly Modeling and Service Denial

⸻

INTERROGATORIES

Interrogatory No. 1: Identify all Palantir datasets, analytic models, or anomaly tags that track or reference housing instability, address changes, or tenancy duration of approximately twelve (12) months or less.

Interrogatory No. 2: Identify all Palantir datasets, analytic models, or anomaly tags that track or reference employment instability, job tenure, or work assignments lasting approximately twelve (12) months or less.

Interrogatory No. 3: State whether Palantir maintains records of Plaintiff’s residential address changes or employment history and, if so, describe whether these were flagged as anomalous.

Interrogatory No. 4: Identify all Palantir clients (governmental or private) that received reports, dashboards, or analytic outputs concerning housing or employment instability patterns between 2000 and the present.

Interrogatory No. 5: State whether Palantir has ever classified Plaintiff’s housing or employment instability as a behavioral futures market signal or used it as justification for attrition, denial of services, or predictive modeling.

Interrogatory No. 6: Identify all Palantir personnel or contractors who accessed or modified records concerning Plaintiff’s housing or employment changes.

Interrogatory No. 7: State whether Palantir has any policy, protocol, or compliance guideline concerning the use of disability-related housing or employment instability in anomaly detection or service denial.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION

Request for Production No. 1: Produce all Palantir records, dashboards, or datasets referencing Plaintiff’s housing changes or tenancy history.

Request for Production No. 2: Produce all Palantir records, dashboards, or datasets referencing Plaintiff’s employment history, job tenure, or job separations.

Request for Production No. 3: Produce all Palantir anomaly tags, watchlist entries, or behavioral futures market analyses that reference patterns of approximately 12-month housing or employment instability.

Request for Production No. 4: Produce all communications (emails, chats, memos) within Palantir that reference Plaintiff’s housing or employment changes.

Request for Production No. 5: Produce all contracts, deliverables, or analytic products provided to clients that reference Plaintiff’s housing or employment instability.

Request for Production No. 6: Produce all compliance, ethics, or legal reviews concerning the use of housing or employment instability as an anomaly or behavioral futures market variable.

Request for Production No. 7: Produce all audit logs or custodial metadata showing Palantir account access to Plaintiff’s housing or employment records.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION

Request for Admission No. 1: Admit that Palantir maintains records of Plaintiff’s housing history, including multiple address changes.

Request for Admission No. 2: Admit that Palantir maintains records of Plaintiff’s employment history, including multiple short-term positions.

Request for Admission No. 3: Admit that Palantir flagged Plaintiff’s housing instability as anomalous or predictive in behavioral futures market modeling.

Request for Admission No. 4: Admit that Palantir flagged Plaintiff’s employment instability as anomalous or predictive in behavioral futures market modeling.

Request for Admission No. 5: Admit that Palantir linked Plaintiff’s housing and employment instability to denial of services, attrition, or continuity-theater operations.

Request for Admission No. 6: Admit that Palantir has not disclosed all records in its possession concerning Plaintiff’s housing and employment instability.

Request for Admission No. 7: Admit that Palantir does not have a policy prohibiting the use of disability-related housing or employment instability patterns in anomaly modeling or behavioral futures markets.
