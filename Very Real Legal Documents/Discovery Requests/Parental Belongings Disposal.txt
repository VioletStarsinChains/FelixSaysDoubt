PLAINTIFF’S FIRST SET OF REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION TO DEFENDANTS

Pursuant to Rule 34 of the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure, Plaintiff Kellyn Clay hereby requests that Defendants produce the following documents and information within their possession, custody, or control:
	1.	Disposal of Plaintiff’s Personal Property
	•	All documents, receipts, or communications relating to the disposal of Plaintiff’s personal property formerly stored in the attic of the Clay family residence at 2715 Collins Drive, Conway, AR 72034.
	•	Identify the specific method(s) of disposal (e.g., trash service, donation, resale, transfer, storage, destruction).
	•	Identify any third-party entities (waste management companies, donation centers, consignment shops, storage facilities, or government agencies) involved in the disposal, handling, or subsequent possession of these items.
	•	Produce all communications between family members, property handlers, or third parties regarding the decision to discard these belongings.
	•	Produce all records sufficient to identify whether Plaintiff’s discarded belongings were transferred, voluntarily or involuntarily, into any data-collection, recycling, or surveillance-related programs.
	2.	Chain of Custody and Location Data
	•	All bills of lading, invoices, manifests, pickup records, or service contracts relating to removal of property from the Clay family residence between 2014 and 2016.
	•	All internal family emails, text messages, or other communications reflecting discussion of Plaintiff’s belongings and their disposal.
	•	All correspondence with local authorities, housing agencies, or contractors relating to attic clear-outs or disposal events at the Clay residence.
	3.	Preservation and Replication
	•	If any of Plaintiff’s personal property was photographed, digitized, or otherwise copied before disposal, produce those materials in full.
	•	Identify whether any of Plaintiff’s personal documents, writings, or artworks were retained by family members, contractors, or other parties.

DEFINITIONS
	•	“Personal property” includes but is not limited to: clothing, books, documents, photographs, electronics, art supplies, journals, or storage boxes belonging to Plaintiff.
	•	“Disposal” includes any form of discarding, transfer, sale, destruction, digitization, or donation.
	•	“Third parties” includes waste management services, municipal services, non-profits, resale shops, private contractors, data brokers, or government agencies.
