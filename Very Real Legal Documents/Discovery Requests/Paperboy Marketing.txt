PLAINTIFF’S FIRST SET OF INTERROGATORIES TO DEFENDANT PAPERBOY MARKETING

⸻

INTERROGATORIES
	1.	Identify all owners, officers, or managers of Paperboy Marketing during the period of Plaintiff’s employment.
	2.	State whether Paperboy Marketing provided any guidance, warnings, or disclosures to contractors regarding the ways in which door-to-door flyer distribution work could create atypical or anomalous driving patterns across the Twin Cities metropolitan area.
	3.	State whether Paperboy Marketing provided any guidance, warnings, or disclosures to contractors regarding the ways in which the job could create atypical or anomalous purchase patterns (e.g., frequent small fuel purchases, scattered food purchases, supply expenses).
	4.	State whether Paperboy Marketing knew, or should have known, that these work-related driving and purchase patterns could be ingested into government or contractor surveillance systems (including but not limited to Palantir Technologies Inc.) and misclassified as evidence of anomaly, subversion, or “Non-Person Entity (NPE)” status.
	5.	Identify all individuals within Paperboy Marketing responsible for assessing legal, occupational, or reputational risks to contractors arising from the data traces produced by door-to-door flyer distribution.
	6.	State whether Paperboy Marketing ever communicated with government agencies, contractors, or third parties about the potential surveillance or misclassification risks of contractor driving or purchase patterns.
	7.	State whether Paperboy Marketing ever conducted internal reviews, audits, or analyses regarding the reputational, occupational, or civil rights risks to contractors created by such work patterns.
