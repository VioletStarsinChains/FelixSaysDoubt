PLAINTIFF’S FIRST CONSOLIDATED DISCOVERY REQUESTS TO DEFENDANT PALANTIR TECHNOLOGIES

Pursuant to Rules 33, 34, and 36 of the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure, Plaintiff Kellyn Clay hereby serves the following Interrogatories, Requests for Production, and Requests for Admissions on Defendant Palantir Technologies Inc., to be answered and responded to fully and under oath within thirty (30) days of service hereof.

⸻

DEFINITIONS
	1.	“Plaintiff” refers to Kellyn Clay.
	2.	“Air Force” refers to the United States Air Force.
	3.	“Air National Guard” refers to any state or federal component of the Air National Guard of the United States.
	4.	“Palantir” refers to Palantir Technologies Inc., its subsidiaries, contractors, and agents.
	5.	“Research datasets” include, but are not limited to, datasets involving medical, behavioral, neurological, biological, or experimental human-subject information.
	6.	“Documents” and “communications” are defined as in Fed. R. Civ. P. 34(a).

⸻

REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION
	1.	Produce any and all documents, communications, datasets, or files maintained by Palantir that reference or involve both Plaintiff and the Air Force in the context of research, experimentation, or human-subject studies.
	2.	Produce any and all documents, communications, datasets, or files maintained by Palantir that reference or involve both Plaintiff and the Air National Guard in the context of research, experimentation, or human-subject studies.
	3.	Produce any and all records in which Plaintiff’s personal data was cross-referenced or federated with biological, medical, behavioral, or neurological datasets ingested by Palantir from the Air Force or Air National Guard.
	4.	Produce any analytic products, dashboards, reports, or anomaly designations generated by Palantir that categorize, label, or otherwise reference Plaintiff as a subject of research, experimentation, or non-consensual study.
	5.	Produce all metadata, audit logs, or access logs showing when, how, and by whom Plaintiff’s personal data was accessed within Air Force or Air National Guard research datasets stored in Palantir platforms.
	6.	Produce any contracts, subcontracts, task orders, or project statements under which Palantir provided services to the Air Force or Air National Guard that involved research, experimentation, or human-subject datasets, from 2010 to present.
	7.	Produce all documents, communications, or internal Palantir records discussing Plaintiff’s exclusion or omission from Air National Guard recruitment datasets, if such exclusion coincided with the use of Plaintiff’s personal data in research or experimentation contexts.
	8.	Produce any correspondence between Palantir and Air Force or Air National Guard officials relating to Plaintiff’s classification, inclusion, or treatment as a subject in research or experimentation.
	9.	Produce all Palantir internal policies, procedures, or guidance documents regarding the ingestion of personal data into military research datasets, with particular reference to consent, non-consent, or informed consent requirements.
	10.	Produce all records, exhibits, or reports referencing Steven Dale Thompson in conjunction with Plaintiff within Air Force or Air National Guard datasets used for research or experimentation.

⸻

INTERROGATORIES
	1.	Identify and describe any datasets, files, or analytic products maintained by Palantir that include Plaintiff’s personal data in connection with Air Force research, experimentation, or human-subject studies.
	2.	Identify and describe any datasets, files, or analytic products maintained by Palantir that include Plaintiff’s personal data in connection with Air National Guard research, experimentation, or human-subject studies.
	3.	For each dataset or file identified in response to Interrogatories 1–2, state:
a. The origin of the dataset (agency, contractor, or research program),
b. The date the dataset was ingested into Palantir systems,
c. The specific Palantir platform(s) in which the dataset was stored or analyzed, and
d. The stated purpose or research application of the dataset.
	4.	Identify any analytic products, dashboards, or reports generated by Palantir that categorized, flagged, or otherwise referenced Plaintiff as a subject in research or experimentation contexts.
	5.	Identify all Palantir employees, contractors, or government clients who accessed, queried, or modified any record involving Plaintiff in connection with Air Force or Air National Guard research datasets, and state the dates of such access.
	6.	State whether Palantir has linked Plaintiff’s personal data to datasets involving biological, behavioral, neurological, or medical information, and if so, describe the context and purpose of such linkage.
	7.	Describe any contracts, subcontracts, or task orders under which Palantir provided services involving Air Force or Air National Guard research datasets from 2010 to the present, including whether such services involved human-subject data.
	8.	Identify and describe any instances where Plaintiff’s data was cross-referenced or “federated” with datasets used for research or experimentation by the Air Force or Air National Guard.
	9.	State whether Palantir’s systems generated anomaly, compliance, or experimental subject designations in relation to Plaintiff, and if so, describe the content and context of those designations.
	10.	Describe Palantir’s policies and procedures regarding the ingestion of personal data into military research datasets, including how consent or non-consent of the data subject is addressed.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR ADMISSIONS
	1.	Admit that Palantir has ingested, maintained, or analyzed datasets originating from the Air Force that contain personal data of Plaintiff.
	2.	Admit that Palantir has ingested, maintained, or analyzed datasets originating from the Air National Guard that contain personal data of Plaintiff.
	3.	Admit that Plaintiff’s personal data has been included in any dataset used for military research, experimentation, or human-subject studies within Palantir platforms.
	4.	Admit that Palantir has generated analytic products categorizing or labeling Plaintiff in connection with Air Force or Air National Guard datasets.
	5.	Admit that Palantir has cross-referenced Plaintiff’s personal data with behavioral, medical, or biological datasets maintained by or for the Air Force or Air National Guard.
	6.	Admit that Palantir has ingested data relating to Steven Dale Thompson, and that Plaintiff’s data appears in proximity to or in conjunction with that data.
	7.	Admit that Palantir has provided analytic outputs involving Plaintiff to the Air Force or Air National Guard, directly or through contractors.
	8.	Admit that Palantir has generated anomaly, risk, or compliance designations referencing Plaintiff within Air Force or Air National Guard datasets.
	9.	Admit that Palantir has contracted with the Air Force or Air National Guard to conduct research or analysis involving human subjects without their knowledge or consent.
	10.	Admit that Plaintiff’s exclusion from Air National Guard recruitment records does not preclude the use of Plaintiff’s data in other Air Force or Air National Guard datasets ingested by Palantir.
