PLAINTIFF’S SIXTEENTH SET OF DISCOVERY REQUESTS TO DEFENDANT PALANTIR TECHNOLOGIES

Plaintiff: Kellyn Clay
Defendant: Palantir Technologies Inc.
Subject Matter: Historical Records of Waterborne Additives Targeted at Anomaly-Classified Individuals

⸻

INTERROGATORIES
	1.	Identify all datasets or archives ingested by Palantir that contain records of waterborne chemical additive programs directed at individuals classified as anomalies prior to Palantir’s incorporation.
	2.	Identify whether Palantir has modeled, tagged, or otherwise analyzed historical datasets concerning targeted additives in residential water systems, including showering exposure.
	3.	Identify all federal, state, or municipal agencies from which Palantir obtained historical data relating to anomaly-based water additive programs.
	4.	Identify whether Palantir linked Plaintiff’s anomaly classification to waterborne additive exposure data originating before Palantir’s founding.
	5.	Identify all Palantir personnel, contractors, or teams who have accessed or worked with such historical datasets concerning waterborne additives.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION
	1.	Produce all historical records in Palantir’s possession concerning waterborne chemical additives targeted at anomaly-classified individuals prior to Palantir’s incorporation.
	2.	Produce all communications, reports, or presentations prepared by Palantir referencing historical anomaly-classification programs involving waterborne additives.
	3.	Produce all records showing whether Plaintiff’s anomaly classification was cross-referenced with historical datasets of water additive exposures.
	4.	Produce all records showing whether Palantir linked showering behaviors or water avoidance behaviors to anomaly classification in Plaintiff’s digital twin.
	5.	Produce all Palantir analyses or dashboards referencing water additive programs as precursors to Palantir’s own anomaly detection or attrition models.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION
	1.	Admit that Palantir ingested historical datasets concerning waterborne chemical additive programs targeted at anomaly-classified individuals.
	2.	Admit that Palantir has modeled or analyzed such historical data within its platforms.
	3.	Admit that Palantir linked Plaintiff’s anomaly classification to historical datasets of waterborne additive exposure.
	4.	Admit that Palantir currently maintains records of water additive programs predating its own incorporation.
	5.	Admit that Palantir has not disclosed to Plaintiff its possession or use of such historical records.
