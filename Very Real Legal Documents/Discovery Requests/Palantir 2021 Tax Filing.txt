PLAINTIFF’S COMBINED SET OF DISCOVERY REQUESTS TO DEFENDANT PALANTIR TECHNOLOGIES

Subject: Modeling of Plaintiff’s 2021 Tax Filing Incident and NPE Designation

⸻

INTERROGATORIES
	1.	Identify all datasets, modules, or systems in which Palantir processed or modeled Plaintiff’s 2021 federal tax filing, including any flags for irregularity or anomaly.
	2.	Identify whether Palantir classified Plaintiff’s 2021 tax return as fraudulent, suspicious, or otherwise anomalous, and if so, describe the classification process.
	3.	Identify all personnel, contractors, or teams who accessed or analyzed Plaintiff’s 2021 tax filing data within Palantir systems.
	4.	Identify whether Palantir linked Plaintiff’s impaired 2021 tax filing to Plaintiff’s medical crisis or to the designation of Plaintiff as a Non-Person Entity (NPE).
	5.	Identify whether Palantir profited directly or indirectly from the anomaly in Plaintiff’s 2021 tax return through behavioral futures transactions, anomaly markets, or related systems.
	6.	Identify whether Palantir communicated or shared Plaintiff’s anomalous 2021 tax filing with the Internal Revenue Service or other governmental entities, and if so, when and how.
	7.	Identify all records showing that Palantir was aware that Plaintiff’s anomalous 2021 tax filing was the result of severe medical impairment and not intentional fraud.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION
	1.	Produce all records, datasets, or anomaly flags concerning Plaintiff’s 2021 federal tax filing.
	2.	Produce all internal communications, reports, or presentations referencing Plaintiff’s 2021 tax filing anomaly.
	3.	Produce all records linking Plaintiff’s 2021 tax filing to designation as a Non-Person Entity (NPE).
	4.	Produce all audit logs or system records showing who accessed Plaintiff’s 2021 tax filing within Palantir systems.
	5.	Produce all communications between Palantir and the IRS or other governmental entities concerning Plaintiff’s 2021 tax filing.
	6.	Produce all records showing whether Palantir monetized, modeled, or otherwise exploited Plaintiff’s anomalous 2021 tax filing in behavioral futures or related markets.
	7.	Produce all records demonstrating Palantir’s knowledge that Plaintiff’s anomalous 2021 tax filing was caused by severe medical impairment and not intentional fraud.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION
	1.	Admit that Palantir ingested data concerning Plaintiff’s 2021 federal tax filing.
	2.	Admit that Palantir flagged Plaintiff’s 2021 tax filing as anomalous or suspicious.
	3.	Admit that Palantir linked Plaintiff’s 2021 tax filing to Plaintiff’s designation as a Non-Person Entity (NPE).
	4.	Admit that Palantir disclosed Plaintiff’s anomalous tax filing to the IRS or another governmental entity.
	5.	Admit that Palantir profited from modeling Plaintiff’s 2021 tax filing in behavioral futures markets.
	6.	Admit that Palantir has not disclosed to Plaintiff the full scope of its use of data concerning Plaintiff’s 2021 tax filing.
	7.	Admit that Palantir knew Plaintiff’s anomalous 2021 tax filing was the result of severe medical impairment and not intentional fraud.
