PLAINTIFF’S REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION OF DOCUMENTS
To: Anthropic, PBC

Pursuant to Rules 26 and 34 of the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure, Plaintiff Kellyn Clay respectfully requests that Defendant produce the following documents and materials in its possession, custody, or control within thirty (30) days of service:
	1.	All documents, communications, and records relating to Plaintiff’s job application(s) to Anthropic, including but not limited to application materials, correspondence, interview notes, and applicant tracking system entries.
	2.	Any automated system prompts, alerts, or internal annotations generated in connection with Plaintiff’s application, including but not limited to filters, flags, keyword matches, or algorithmic scoring outputs.
	3.	Any internal communications or documents referencing system-generated outputs associated with Plaintiff’s application.
	4.	Policies, guidelines, or procedural manuals in effect at the time of Plaintiff’s application governing the handling of applicants who are flagged or filtered by automated systems.
	5.	Any logs, metadata, or system documentation showing when, how, and why Plaintiff’s application was processed, flagged, or otherwise dispositioned.

⸻

Instructions and Definitions
	•	“Documents” shall include but not be limited to emails, text messages, Slack messages, database records, system logs, automated prompts, or any electronically stored information.
	•	“System prompts” refers to any automated text, flag, notice, or classification generated by Anthropic’s internal or third-party systems during the application process.
	•	This request does not allege wrongdoing, but seeks transparency into the processes that affected Plaintiff’s candidacy.
