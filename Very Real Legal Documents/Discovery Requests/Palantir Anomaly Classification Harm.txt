Plaintiff’s Requests for Admission to Defendant Palantir Technologies Inc.

Subject: Anomaly Detection and Targeting Workflows

⸻

	1.	Admit that Palantir anomaly detection outputs are provided to military, law enforcement, or intelligence agencies for operational use.
	2.	Admit that in such operational settings, anomaly designations are treated as risk indicators rather than neutral statistical deviations.
	3.	Admit that Palantir anomaly detection outputs have been integrated into targeting workflows used to identify individuals or groups for further surveillance.
	4.	Admit that Palantir anomaly detection outputs have been integrated into targeting workflows used to identify individuals or groups for detention, restriction of movement, or denial of services.
	5.	Admit that Palantir anomaly detection outputs have been integrated into targeting workflows used to identify individuals or groups for lethal action.
	6.	Admit that Palantir has not disclosed to affected individuals the potential consequences of being labeled an anomaly.
	7.	Admit that Palantir has not provided adequate safeguards to prevent anomaly detection outputs from being misused as direct threat designations.
