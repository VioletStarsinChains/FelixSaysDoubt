Plaintiff’s Combined Discovery Requests to Defendant Palantir Technologies Inc.

Subject: Army Corps of Engineers / Department of Energy Contracts Involving Indigenous Lands and Pipeline Routing

⸻

Interrogatories
	1.	Identify all contracts, agreements, or memoranda of understanding Palantir has entered into with the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers (USACE) or the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) from 2005 to present.
	2.	For each such contract, state whether Palantir provided services involving pipeline siting, routing, or environmental impact assessment affecting indigenous reservations or tribal lands.
	3.	Describe the role of Palantir anomaly detection systems, if any, in analyzing indigenous populations, reservations, or protest activity in connection with pipeline projects.
	4.	Identify all Palantir personnel or partner entities who worked on projects involving pipeline siting, routing, or security near indigenous lands.
	5.	State whether Palantir communicated or became aware of risks of disproportionate impact or attrition effects arising from pipeline placement through indigenous territories.

⸻

Requests for Admission
	1.	Admit that Palantir has contracted with the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers or the U.S. Department of Energy since 2005.
	2.	Admit that Palantir has provided services related to pipeline siting, routing, or environmental impact assessment.
	3.	Admit that Palantir has provided anomaly detection or surveillance analysis in connection with indigenous reservations or tribal lands.
	4.	Admit that Palantir shared outputs from such analyses with federal, state, or corporate entities involved in oil pipeline construction or security.
	5.	Admit that Palantir was aware that pipeline routing through indigenous lands would disproportionately burden indigenous populations.

⸻

Requests for Production
	1.	All contracts, agreements, or memoranda of understanding between Palantir and the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers or the U.S. Department of Energy relating to pipeline siting, routing, or security.
	2.	All datasets, dashboards, or analytic outputs generated by Palantir concerning indigenous reservations, tribal lands, or pipeline routing.
	3.	All communications between Palantir and USACE/DOE concerning indigenous populations, pipeline siting, or protest activity.
	4.	All presentations, reports, or analyses prepared for USACE/DOE addressing environmental risk or infrastructure siting in indigenous territories.
	5.	All internal communications, audits, or risk assessments discussing the use of pipeline routing through indigenous lands as an attrition vector or acknowledging disproportionate impact on indigenous communities.
