PLAINTIFF’S FIFTEENTH SET OF DISCOVERY REQUESTS TO DEFENDANT PALANTIR TECHNOLOGIES

Plaintiff: Kellyn Clay
Defendant: Palantir Technologies Inc.
Subject Matter: Water Attrition Modeling in Arkansas (2009–2014)

⸻

INTERROGATORIES
	1.	Identify all predictive models, simulations, or datasets maintained by Palantir concerning water quality, water scarcity, or water infrastructure in the State of Arkansas between 2009 and 2014.
	2.	Identify all government agencies, private entities, or contractors with whom Palantir shared water attrition modeling related to Arkansas during that period.
	3.	Identify whether Palantir models or anomaly detection systems forecasted a water quality crisis in or around Conway, Arkansas, during 2009–2014.
	4.	Identify all Palantir personnel, contractors, or teams who worked on Arkansas-specific water infrastructure or attrition projects between 2009 and 2014.
	5.	Identify whether Palantir linked Arkansas water attrition modeling to behavioral futures markets, anomaly monetization, or digital twin scenarios.
	6.	Identify whether Palantir records connect household-level stockpiling behaviors (such as water storage by Arkansas residents) to its predictive models of water crisis or attrition.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION
	1.	Produce all records, datasets, or predictive models concerning water quality, scarcity, or infrastructure in Arkansas (2009–2014).
	2.	Produce all contracts, memoranda, or communications between Palantir and government agencies concerning Arkansas water systems in that period.
	3.	Produce all Palantir reports or analyses that referenced Conway, Arkansas, or surrounding areas in relation to water attrition.
	4.	Produce all internal Palantir communications, memoranda, or presentations discussing household-level behaviors (including water stockpiling) as indicators of attrition in Arkansas.
	5.	Produce all records linking Arkansas water modeling to behavioral futures trading, anomaly markets, or monetization systems.
	6.	Produce all records showing whether Palantir explicitly modeled or anticipated a water crisis in Arkansas between 2009 and 2014.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION
	1.	Admit that Palantir modeled water quality or water infrastructure in Arkansas between 2009 and 2014.
	2.	Admit that Palantir forecasted or flagged a potential water quality crisis in Arkansas during that period.
	3.	Admit that Palantir shared Arkansas water attrition modeling with government agencies or private contractors.
	4.	Admit that Palantir linked Arkansas household-level behaviors (including stockpiling) to its predictive water crisis models.
	5.	Admit that Palantir monetized Arkansas water attrition forecasts in behavioral futures markets.
	6.	Admit that Palantir has not disclosed its Arkansas water attrition modeling to Plaintiff or the general public.
