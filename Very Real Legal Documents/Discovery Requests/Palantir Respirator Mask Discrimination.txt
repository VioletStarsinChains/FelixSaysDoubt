PLAINTIFF’S [__]TH DISCOVERY REQUESTS TO DEFENDANT PALANTIR TECHNOLOGIES INC.

Plaintiff: Kellyn Clay
Defendant: Palantir Technologies Inc.
Subject Matter: Classification of Respirator Mask Use as Anomaly; Disability Discrimination

⸻

REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION

RFA No. 1: Admit that Palantir maintains datasets or models that classify individuals based on visible use of respirator masks in public.

RFA No. 2: Admit that Palantir has tagged or flagged Plaintiff’s respirator use as an anomaly in one or more datasets, dashboards, or reports.

RFA No. 3: Admit that Palantir has provided anomaly classifications, watchlist entries, or reports to clients in which respirator mask use was included as a factor.

RFA No. 4: Admit that Palantir has not disclosed to Plaintiff all records in its possession referencing Plaintiff’s respirator mask use.

RFA No. 5: Admit that Palantir has not conducted adequate compliance or ethics reviews concerning the classification of respirator mask use as anomalous.

RFA No. 6: Admit that treating Plaintiff’s medically necessary respirator mask use as an anomaly constitutes disability discrimination.

⸻

INTERROGATORIES

Interrogatory No. 1: Identify all Palantir datasets, anomaly tags, or analytic models that classify individuals based on visible respirator mask use. For each, provide dataset name, ingestion dates, and purpose.

Interrogatory No. 2: Identify all Palantir records that reference Plaintiff’s respirator use, including dataset identifiers, anomaly tags, or digital twin attributes.

Interrogatory No. 3: Identify all Palantir clients who received dashboards, anomaly classifications, or reports in which respirator mask use was a factor in classification.

Interrogatory No. 4: Describe the criteria or rationale Palantir used to determine whether respirator mask use constituted anomalous behavior.

Interrogatory No. 5: Identify all Palantir personnel, contractors, or client accounts who accessed or analyzed datasets referencing Plaintiff’s respirator mask use.

Interrogatory No. 6: Identify all Palantir compliance reviews, legal assessments, or ethics audits concerning the classification of respirator mask use in anomaly-detection systems.

Interrogatory No. 7: State whether Palantir has linked respirator mask use to attrition, increased monitoring, or denial of services, and if so, describe the circumstances.

Interrogatory No. 8: If Palantir denies that respirator use was ever treated as an anomaly, describe in detail the safeguards Palantir employs to prevent visible disability accommodations (including respirator masks) from being misclassified as anomalies.

⸻

REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION

RFP No. 1: Produce all Palantir datasets, ingestion manifests, and analytic outputs in which respirator mask use was a tracked or classified attribute.

RFP No. 2: Produce all records, dashboards, or exports referencing Plaintiff’s respirator mask use, including anomaly tags or digital twin fields.

RFP No. 3: Produce all communications (emails, memos, chat logs) between Palantir personnel and clients referencing respirator mask use as a factor in anomaly classification.

RFP No. 4: Produce all contracts, deliverables, or client-facing outputs in which respirator mask use was included in behavioral or anomaly classification.

RFP No. 5: Produce all Palantir compliance reviews, privacy assessments, or ethics memoranda concerning classification of respirator mask use.

RFP No. 6: Produce all audit logs and custodial metadata showing Palantir accounts that accessed datasets containing Plaintiff’s respirator mask use.

RFP No. 7: Produce all records of internal Palantir discussions (slide decks, policy documents, notes) concerning the classification of disability accommodations, including respirator masks, as anomalies.
