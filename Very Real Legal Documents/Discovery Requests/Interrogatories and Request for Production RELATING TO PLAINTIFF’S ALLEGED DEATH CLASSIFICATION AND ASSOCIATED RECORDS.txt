INTERROGATORIES

Interrogatory No. 1:
Have you or any agency or contractor acting on your behalf ever classified, recorded, or represented Plaintiff Kellyn Clay as deceased, either internally or externally?

Interrogatory No. 2:
If your response to Interrogatory No. 1 is yes, please identify the date, jurisdiction, and originating party for any such classification, as well as the reasoning or source data used to support it.

Interrogatory No. 3:
Has Plaintiff’s alleged status as deceased ever been used as a basis for:
	•	Terminating benefits;
	•	Restricting civil rights;
	•	Reclassifying Plaintiff as a Non-Person Entity (NPE);
	•	Redirecting mail or digital communications;
	•	Creating synthetic identities or proxies?

If yes, please provide specific details.

Interrogatory No. 4:
Has any internal or external system, database, or modeling software (including but not limited to Palantir, DHS biometrics, SSA death master file, state-level DMV or public health systems) recorded Plaintiff as deceased?

Interrogatory No. 5:
Have you notified any third party (including foreign or private entities) of Plaintiff’s death, legal incapacitation, or other status alteration based on such a classification?

REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION

Request for Production No. 1:
Produce any and all documents, databases, screenshots, or system outputs that refer to or contain a record of Plaintiff Kellyn Clay being classified as deceased, from any time period.

	•	Communications with SSA, CDC, local or state health departments;

Request for Production No. 3:
Produce any records showing use of a synthetic identity, proxy, avatar, or digital twin that replaced or stood in for Plaintiff in any public or private-facing system.

Request for Production No. 4:
Produce all audit logs, metadata, or access records for any event in which Plaintiff’s status as alive or deceased was changed, altered, or queried within a government, intelligence, or contractor system.

Request for Production No. 5:
Produce any and all documents related to the origin and propagation of any false report of Plaintiff’s death, including communications with news agencies, obituaries, automated notices, or modeling platforms.
