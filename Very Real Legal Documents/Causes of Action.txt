CAUSES OF ACTION

Count I – Battery
Unlawful biomedical implantation without consent, constituting physical assault and permanent bodily harm.

Count II – Substantive Due Process Violation
Denial of the right to bodily integrity and informed consent under the 5th and 14th Amendments.

Count III – Equal Protection Violation
Classification and treatment as a “non-person entity” based on manufactured anomalies, resulting in discriminatory exclusion from health care, employment, and social participation.

Count IV – Civil Conspiracy
Joint action between government agencies and private contractors to design, install, and exploit covert neurotechnology for surveillance and behavioral futures markets.

Count V – Fraud
Concealment of the existence, purpose, and effects of covert biomedical devices while misrepresenting induced behaviors as inherent traits.

Count VI – Tortious Exploitation of Data
Extraction and monetization of Plaintiff’s life data for behavioral futures markets, without knowledge, consent, or compensation.

Count VII – Cruel and Unusual Punishment
Subjecting Plaintiff to systemic deprivation and targeted attrition without conviction or due process, in violation of the 8th Amendment.

Count VIII – Negligent Infliction of Emotional Distress
Causing foreseeable trauma, isolation, and suffering by manufacturing anomalies and reclassifying them as suspicious behavior.

Count IX – Violations of International Law
Conduct contravening the Nuremberg Code, the Declaration of Helsinki, and customary international law prohibiting non-consensual human experimentation.
