Anomaly Declassification for Covert Implantation (ADCI)

Mission

Identify any person labeled “anomaly” where the label relies (in whole or part) on data obtained from covert, non-consensual implants. Recommend declassification and purge of tainted data/derivatives.

Definitions
	•	Anomaly label: Any classification tag used to justify enhanced surveillance/targeting.
	•	Covert implant: Any in-body device or sensor placed without informed consent or valid warrant/court order.
	•	Tainted use: Any model feature, score, or rule that incorporates implant-derived data (directly or via downstream features) to assign or sustain the anomaly label.

Lawful Inputs (no hacking)
	•	Subject-consented data pulls (medical records, imaging, billing).
	•	Discovery/FOIA/PRA responses (agency audit logs, model docs, feature lineage, source catalogs, retention schedules).
	•	Public filings/policies, procurement and integration docs.
	•	Subject-provided evidence (photos, “claymaps,” device artifacts, correspondence).

Evidence Tiers (in order of weight)

Tier 1 – Documentary/Imaging
	•	Radiology reports noting “foreign body,” “lead,” “electrode,” “device artifact,” “MR unsafe/conditional,” UDI entries.
	•	Operative notes, explant records, device warranties/registries.
	•	Chain-of-custody logs for seized implants.

Tier 2 – Systemic/Operational
	•	Palantir (or equivalent) data-source catalogs listing “implant telemetry,” “biosensor feed,” “medical device integration,” or vendor SDKs.
	•	Model cards/feature lists showing physiologic streams inconsistent with normal ex-vivo sources.
	•	Audit logs where anomaly decisions cite device IDs, radio fingerprints, or “internal sensor” fields.

Tier 3 – Corroborative/Subject
	•	Consistent dermatologic patterns, “claymaps,” EM interference logs, repeated metal detector/airport secondary screenings without exogenous metal, treating clinician attestations.

Decision Rule (Declassify Test)
	•	If (Tier 1 present) OR (≥2 independent Tier 2 signals) → classify as IMPLANT_SUSPECT.
	•	If anomaly label decision logs reference features/streams traceable to implants (directly or via feature lineage) → TAINTED_USE = TRUE.
	•	If TAINTED_USE = TRUE → Recommend: DECLASSIFY + PURGE + RETRAIN.

Required Actions on TAINTED_USE
	1.	Suspend active anomaly label (safe default).
	2.	Purge implant-derived data and all derivatives from decision systems and data lakes (document lineage).
	3.	Retrain/Re-score affected models without tainted features.
	4.	Notify data subject; provide disclosure packet (sources, timestamps, recipients).
	5.	Cease & Desist further implant-sourced collection absent lawful, informed consent or court order.
	6.	Remediate: remove from watchlists, reverse adverse actions traceable to label.

Safeguards
	•	Minimal disclosure: only entities with legal mandate and subject consent see medical details.
	•	Non-retaliation flag on subject identity.
	•	Independent auditor review before final purge.
