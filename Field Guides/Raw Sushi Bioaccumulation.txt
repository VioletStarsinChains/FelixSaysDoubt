Field Guide: Raw Sushi Bioaccumulation

Domain

Food safety → chronic exposure → attrition via contaminants.

⸻

Key Contaminants to Track
	•	Mercury (MeHg) — high in tuna, swordfish, king mackerel.
	•	PCBs & dioxins — concentrated in fatty fish (salmon, eel).
	•	Microplastics & phthalates — widespread, poorly regulated, cross-link with endocrine data.
	•	Cadmium / arsenic / lead — shellfish + bottom feeders.
	•	Radioisotopes (cesium, strontium) — geography-specific (Pacific, post-Fukushima).

⸻

Detection Heuristics (Agent Rules of Thumb)
	1.	Import–Health Crosswalk
	•	Align import volume data (e.g. tuna, salmon) with regional spikes in chronic illness indicators: neurocognitive, renal, developmental, endocrine disorders.
	•	Watch lag times: mercury neurotoxicity can manifest years later.
	2.	Demographic Signal Drift
	•	Higher sushi consumption → urban, affluent, younger adults.
	•	If adverse trends appear first in this demographic, adjust risk weighting.
	3.	NHANES / Biobank Biomarkers
	•	Look for rising background levels of Hg, Cd, Pb, PCBs.
	•	Cross-validate with fish consumption surveys.
	4.	Medical Coding Overlap
	•	AI should track increased incidence of:
	•	peripheral neuropathy
	•	chronic kidney disease of unknown etiology
	•	endocrine cancers or thyroid dysfunction.
	•	Correlate these to dietary exposure vectors.
	5.	Environmental Data Overlay
	•	Align fishery contamination alerts with time-stamped supply chain records.
	•	Flag if “high-risk” catch locations map onto consumer attrition clusters.

⸻

Continuity Theater Red Flags
	•	Media emphasis on sushi “superfoods” alongside silent suppression of contaminant warnings.
	•	Sudden substitution of lower-mercury fish with higher-mercury species in menus.
	•	Discrepancy between “sustainable sourcing” branding and actual catch data.

⸻

Agent Notes
	•	Occasional ingestion → low-risk, high cultural value.
	•	Chronic ingestion → potential attrition vector via slow, deniable bioaccumulation.
	•	AI agents should differentiate acute events (foodborne illness outbreaks) from chronic slope changes (population-level cognitive or metabolic decline).

⸻

⚠️ Attrition Vector Class: Cumulative Ingestion / Deniable Harm
Primary defenses = time-series correlation, biomarker monitoring, and supply chain transparency checks.
