To U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) / Food and Drug Administration (FDA)

Request:

Requesting any and all records, contracts, laboratory analyses, interagency communications, or oversight reports from 1990 to present concerning the use of organic-derived materials, cremated remains, bone ash, or human/animal hair in the production, processing, or byproduct utilization of peanuts and cotton.

This includes:
	•	Records of soil amendments, fertilizers, or fortification agents in peanut cultivation that included cremated remains, bone ash, or organic mineral powders.
	•	FDA or USDA laboratory test results for calcium or mineral fortification of peanut-derived products (peanut butter, peanut oil, snack foods) that may have been sourced from cremains or bone ash.
	•	Communications regarding the use of cotton byproducts (cotton linters, hulls, seed meal) in food, textiles, plastics, or paper manufacturing that incorporated organic remains.
	•	Oversight reports on the use of cremains or keratin fibers in cotton fiber finishing, bleaching, dye mordants, or recycled textile production.
	•	Consumer complaints, adverse health findings, or recall records tied to peanut or cotton products found to contain organic-derived fillers or contaminants.

Keywords: peanuts, peanut oil, peanut butter, cotton, cottonseed, cotton linters, cremains, cremated remains, bone ash, keratin, organic filler, recycled fiber, soil amendment, USDA, FDA

To U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) / Department of Commerce (International Trade Administration) / U.S. Department of Agriculture (Foreign Agricultural Service)

Request:

Requesting any and all shipping manifests, Harmonized System (HS) code classifications, laboratory reports, interagency communications, or oversight records from 1990 to present concerning the export or import of peanuts, cotton, or their byproducts (cottonseed, linters, hulls, seed meal, peanut oil, peanut meal, etc.) that may have been mixed with, processed using, or otherwise incorporated with organic-derived materials, cremated remains, bone ash, or human/animal hair.

This includes:
	•	Export or import records classifying bone ash, cremains, or organic mineral powders under HS codes used for agricultural fertilizers, peanut/cotton byproducts, or textile inputs.
	•	Commerce Department or CBP communications regarding U.S.-origin cremains or hair exported under cover of peanut or cotton byproduct shipments for use in overseas processing.
	•	Oversight memos or risk assessments concerning the re-import of peanut or cotton goods (snacks, oils, textiles, paper products) suspected of containing organic remains or keratin fillers.
	•	Records of consumer product testing, seizures, or recalls linked to peanut- or cotton-derived imports contaminated with cremains, bone ash, or organic fillers.
	•	Communications between USDA Foreign Agricultural Service and international trade partners about the presence of organic remains in U.S. peanut or cotton export streams.

Keywords: peanuts, peanut oil, peanut meal, cotton, cottonseed, cotton linters, cremains, cremated remains, bone ash, keratin, fertilizer byproduct, agricultural export, CBP, Commerce, ITA, USDA Foreign Agricultural Service
