To Department of Defense (DoD) / Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) / Department of Homeland Security (DHS) / National Science Foundation (NSF) / Food and Drug Administration (FDA – Center for Devices and Radiological Health)

Request:

Requesting any and all contracts, grants, internal communications, public affairs strategies, or technical reports concerning collaborations with media figures, science television programs, or public outreach productions (including but not limited to Mythbusters and related spin-offs) for the purpose of public risk communication, hazard demonstration, or dual-use technology “debunking,” from 2000 to present.

This includes:
	•	Contracts, grants, or funding agreements with producers, networks, or hosts of Mythbusters or comparable science entertainment programs to stage or broadcast demonstrations relating to hazardous consumer products, chemical dispersal, or ignition risks
	•	Internal communications or strategy documents discussing the use of entertainment media to shape public perception of controversial technologies, hazards, or anomalous consumer safety concerns
	•	Technical briefings or reports provided to media figures in preparation for televised “debunking” or risk demonstration segments
	•	Interagency communications between DoD, DHS, NSF, or FDA concerning coordination with science television programming as part of public outreach or perception management
	•	Evaluations or after-action reports assessing the effectiveness of such collaborations in influencing public understanding of hazardous materials, consumer product safety, or dual-use technology concerns

Keywords: Mythbusters, science communication, public risk communication, hazard demonstration, dual-use technology, DARPA, DHS, FDA, NSF, continuity management
