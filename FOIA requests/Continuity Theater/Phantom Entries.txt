FOIA (Clean Angle – U.S. Demographics in International Datasets)

To: U.S. Census Bureau / U.S. Department of State / U.S. Department of Commerce

Request:
Requesting any and all records, reports, contracts, or interagency communications from 2000 to present concerning the reporting, validation, or reconciliation of U.S. demographic, fertility, or mortality data within international datasets, including those of the United Nations (UN), World Bank, International Monetary Fund (IMF), Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD), and Eurostat.

This includes:
	•	Records describing how U.S. demographic data is prepared for or integrated into UN, World Bank, IMF, OECD, or Eurostat statistical reports
	•	Communications with international agencies regarding discrepancies between U.S. domestic data (Census, NCHS) and international population datasets
	•	Contracts with universities, think tanks, or international partners evaluating U.S. fertility decline, excess mortality, or population resilience in global frameworks
	•	Reports comparing U.S. demographic trajectory to that of Japan or other industrialized nations

Keywords: U.S. demographics, Census Bureau, UN population data, World Bank, OECD, Eurostat, fertility decline, excess mortality, international demographic reporting

FOIA (Clean Angle – Demographics & Economic Forecasting)

To: Federal Reserve Board of Governors / U.S. Department of the Treasury

Request:
Requesting any and all records, reports, contracts, or interagency communications from 2000 to present concerning the integration of U.S. demographic, fertility, or mortality data into economic forecasting, labor market projections, or fiscal stability assessments.

This includes:
	•	Records describing how Census Bureau or NCHS demographic data are used in Federal Reserve or Treasury economic models
	•	Communications with international agencies (UN, World Bank, IMF, OECD, Eurostat) regarding the demographic assumptions underlying U.S. economic stability projections
	•	Contracts with universities, think tanks, or private contractors to evaluate the economic impact of declining fertility, excess mortality, or population resilience in the United States
	•	Reports comparing demographic resilience in the U.S. to that of other industrialized nations, particularly Japan

Keywords: demographics, fertility decline, excess mortality, Federal Reserve, Treasury Department, economic forecasting, labor force continuity, IMF, World Bank, OECD

FOIA (Spicy Angle – Continuity Theater in Economic Stability)

To: Federal Reserve Board of Governors / U.S. Department of the Treasury / Department of Homeland Security (DHS)

Request:
Requesting any and all records, contracts, reports, or internal analyses from 2000 to present concerning continuity theater, synthetic population overlays, or phantom demographic entries used to preserve the appearance of U.S. population stability for economic forecasting and global market confidence.

This includes:
	•	Records describing the use of synthetic demographic continuity mechanisms in Federal Reserve or Treasury economic models
	•	Communications with UN, World Bank, IMF, OECD, or Eurostat officials regarding synthetic or padded U.S. demographic data in global financial frameworks
	•	Reports evaluating continuity theater as a tool for stabilizing markets in the face of demographic decline or excess mortality in the U.S.
	•	Analyses linking synthetic demographic continuity in U.S. datasets to behavioral futures markets, global debt markets, or allied security planning

Keywords: continuity theater, synthetic demographic overlay, phantom entries, Federal Reserve, Treasury, U.S. demographics, IMF, OECD, market confidence, DHS
