To Department of Transportation (DOT), Federal Highway Administration (FHWA), and DHS (Science & Technology Directorate)

Request:

Requesting any and all documents, contracts, pilot program documentation, or internal communications from 2008 to present concerning the use of navigation applications (including Google Maps, Waze, and Apple Maps) for behavioral compliance testing, predictive analytics, or traffic modeling.

This includes:
	•	Records of federal partnerships or research agreements with navigation app providers to study compliance vs. resistance in response to routing instructions
	•	Documentation of pilot programs analyzing driver decision-making when given alternate routes, detours, or warnings (e.g., accident reports, police presence, congestion alerts)
	•	Contracts or communications with contractors (e.g., Palantir, RAND, Booz Allen) regarding the integration of navigation app behavioral data into predictive modeling platforms
	•	Oversight reports or ethical reviews on the use of navigation app telemetry as a proxy for behavioral compliance or consumer risk profiling
	•	Internal communications concerning the secondary use of navigation app data for law enforcement, predictive policing, or behavioral futures markets

Keywords: Google Maps, Waze, Apple Maps, navigation apps, compliance testing, resistance, predictive modeling, driver behavior, traffic simulation, behavioral futures
