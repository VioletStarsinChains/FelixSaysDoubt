To Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) / U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) / Department of Commerce (International Trade Administration)

Request:

Requesting any and all records, contracts, shipping manifests, Harmonized System (HS) code analyses, laboratory reports, or interagency communications from 1990 to present concerning the export of cremated human remains, bone ash, human hair, or other organic remains classified as “fertilizer,” “ash,” “organic mineral powder,” “keratin,” or similar byproduct categories.

This includes:
	•	Records describing or permitting the classification of cremated remains, hair, or bone ash under HS codes used for fertilizer, animal byproducts, or industrial powders.
	•	Export licenses, manifests, or declarations showing shipments of organic remains for use in foreign textile, plastic, ceramic, or paint manufacturing.
	•	EPA or CBP communications regarding the environmental or public health implications of shipping cremated or organic remains overseas for industrial processing.
	•	Commerce Department or International Trade Administration records of imports of finished consumer goods (rugs, ceramics, plastics, elastics, paints) that may have incorporated U.S.-origin cremated remains or hair as filler, pigment, or polymer additive.
	•	Oversight memos, risk assessments, or consumer product testing tied to supply chains where U.S. exports of cremains or hair returned embedded in manufactured goods.

Keywords: cremains, cremated remains, bone ash, human hair, keratin, fertilizer byproduct, ash exports, organic mineral powder, recycled textile, recycled ceramic, eco-plastic, CBP, EPA, Commerce, ITA
