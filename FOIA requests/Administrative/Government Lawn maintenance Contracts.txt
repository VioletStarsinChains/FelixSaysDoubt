FOIA – Government Landscaping Contracts

To: General Services Administration (GSA) / Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) / U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA)

Request:
Requesting any and all contracts, grants, memoranda, reports, or interagency communications from 1990 to present concerning federally funded landscaping, lawn care, or grounds maintenance services provided to residential properties.

This includes:
	•	Contracts with private landscaping or groundskeeping companies for the upkeep of foreclosed, abandoned, or federally managed residential properties
	•	Records of HUD, VA, or GSA-managed properties receiving routine yard or grounds maintenance
	•	Reports on expenditures for lawn care, tree trimming, or grounds maintenance linked to residential addresses rather than institutional facilities
	•	Communications regarding the visual upkeep of residential properties as a component of community stabilization or neighborhood continuity

Keywords: landscaping contracts, lawn care, abandoned homes, HUD properties, VA properties, GSA, grounds maintenance

To: Department of Homeland Security (DHS – S&T, FEMA) / Department of Defense (DoD – USACE, DARPA) / Intelligence Advanced Research Projects Activity (IARPA)

Request:
Requesting any and all records, contracts, pilots, reports, or internal analyses from 1990 to present concerning the use of landscaping or lawn maintenance contracts as continuity-theater infrastructure for concealing mass attrition or demographic collapse.

This includes:
	•	Records describing federal payment or reimbursement for private landscaping services at residences associated with deceased or missing individuals
	•	Analyses of yard upkeep, lawn mowing, and grounds maintenance as continuity props to preserve the appearance of occupied neighborhoods
	•	Reports on the role of landscaping contractors in continuity-of-operations or behavioral modeling frameworks
	•	Communications between DHS, DoD, or IARPA and private landscaping vendors regarding residential maintenance outside of standard municipal contracts
	•	Risk/benefit assessments of concealing attrition via landscaping continuity programs
