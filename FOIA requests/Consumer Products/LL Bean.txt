To Federal Trade Commission (FTC) / Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) / U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA – Agricultural Marketing Service, Textile & Leather Programs)

Request:

Requesting any and all records, supplier certifications, laboratory analyses, interagency communications, or oversight reports from 1990 to present concerning the use of organic-derived materials, cremated remains, bone ash, human hair, or other human/animal remains in the production of outdoor apparel, footwear, textiles, leather goods, and consumer home products.

This includes:
	•	Records describing or authorizing the substitution of bone ash, cremains, or organic powders as fillers, mordants, dye fixatives, or coatings in textiles, footwear, backpacks, or canvas goods.
	•	Laboratory analyses or supplier certifications identifying organic remains in rubber soles, polymer-based insulation (polyester fills), or coated fabrics used in outdoor apparel and gear.
	•	Oversight reports or consumer safety findings tied to the use of organic-derived tanning agents, mordants, or fillers in leather or textile production for clothing, boots, or accessories.
	•	Records of import inspections or supplier audits identifying organic remains in foreign-manufactured textiles, polymers, or wood-based products (e.g., particle board in furniture) sold under outdoor retail brands.
	•	Communications between FTC, CPSC, USDA, or contractors regarding the safety, legality, or ethical implications of using cremains or organic-derived fillers in outdoor consumer goods.

Keywords: outdoor apparel, textiles, leather, footwear, polymer filler, cremains, cremated remains, bone ash, keratin, recycled organics, consumer safety, FTC, CPSC, USDA
