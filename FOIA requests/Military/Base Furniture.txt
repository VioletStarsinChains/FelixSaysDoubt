To Department of Defense / Defense Logistics Agency (DLA) / Department of the Army Housing / Defense Health Agency (DHA) / Inspector General of the Department of Defense

Request:

Requesting any and all contracts, procurement specifications, supplier audits, laboratory analyses, or internal communications from 1990 to present concerning the use of organic-derived materials, cremated remains, bone ash, human hair, or other human/animal remains in the manufacture of particle board, medium-density fiberboard (MDF), and other composite wood products purchased for Department of Defense housing, barracks, hospitals, offices, or other facilities.

This includes:
	•	DLA contracts or supply specifications permitting or describing the use of recycled fillers or organic byproducts in composite wood products.
	•	Laboratory test results, supplier certifications, or inspector general audits addressing the composition of particle board furniture, cabinetry, or flooring supplied to DoD facilities.
	•	Communications between DLA, DHA, or military housing offices and federal agencies (EPA, CPSC, HUD) regarding the safety, sourcing, or disclosure of organic remains in composite wood.
	•	Records of health complaints, adverse event reports, or housing safety reviews linked to particle board cabinetry or furniture suspected of containing cremated or organic material.
	•	Oversight or contractor communications concerning the import of composite wood products containing cremains or other human/animal remains for use in military housing or medical settings.

Keywords: particle board, MDF, composite wood, cremains, cremated remains, bone ash, keratin filler, recycled organics, DLA procurement, DoD housing, DHA, military supply chain
