FOIA Request: VA Pilots Involving Optical Neurotechnology

To: Department of Veterans Affairs (VA – Office of Research & Development, Veterans Health Administration) / Department of Defense (DoD – DARPA, USAMRDC)

Request:
Requesting any and all records, pilots, contracts, grants, research proposals, or interagency communications from 1990 to present concerning the deployment, testing, or evaluation of optical neurotechnology in veteran populations.

This includes:
	•	Records describing ophthalmic or optometric procedures used as vectors for implantable neurotechnology or neuromodulatory devices in veterans
	•	Reports on VA medical centers or affiliated clinics participating in neurotech pilots funded by DoD, DARPA, or defense contractors
	•	Contracts or collaborations between the VA and private medical device manufacturers (including but not limited to Medtronic) regarding optical/neurotechnology research
	•	Communications concerning the use of routine eye exams or vision-care pathways as opportunities for experimental neurotech deployment
	•	Risk/benefit assessments of applying optical neurotechnology to veterans as part of rehabilitation, prosthetic augmentation, or cognitive performance programs

Keywords: VA, optical neurotechnology, ophthalmic implant, neurotech pilot, DARPA, DoD, Medtronic, vision prosthetics, neuromodulation
