To Department of Defense / Defense Logistics Agency (DLA) / Defense Commissary Agency (DeCA) / Inspector General of the Department of Defense

Request:

Requesting any and all contracts, procurement specifications, supplier audits, laboratory analyses, or internal communications from 1990 to present concerning the use of organic-derived materials, cremated remains, bone ash, human hair, or other human/animal remains in the production, clarification, packaging, or distribution of alcoholic beverages procured for Department of Defense facilities, commissaries, embassies, military exchanges, or Morale, Welfare, and Recreation (MWR) programs.

This includes:
	•	DLA or DeCA procurement contracts for beer, wine, spirits, or single-serving packaged alcohol, including testing results or supplier certifications regarding the use of bone ash, cremains, or organic-derived materials in fermentation, clarification, or packaging.
	•	Laboratory analyses of alcoholic beverages supplied to commissaries, embassies, or base facilities identifying organic fillers, filtration agents, or packaging components derived from cremains.
	•	Communications between DLA, DeCA, or other DoD entities and federal regulators (TTB, FDA) regarding the use of organic remains in alcoholic beverages procured for service members or overseas facilities.
	•	Inspector General audits, oversight reviews, or contractor compliance reports concerning the sourcing of alcohol containing organic-derived fillers or packaging.
	•	Records of complaints, adverse health reports, or safety incidents tied to alcohol supplied through DoD channels later found to incorporate organic remains.

Keywords: alcohol procurement, spirits, beer, wine, single-serving bottles, cremains, cremated remains, bone ash, bone char, keratin, filtration aid, packaging, commissary, DLA, DeCA, DoD supply chain
