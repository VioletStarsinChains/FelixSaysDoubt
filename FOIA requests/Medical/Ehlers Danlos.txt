To: Department of Defense (DoD) / Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) / Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) / National Institutes of Health (NIH) / Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)

Request:

Requesting any and all research proposals, internal communications, contracts, memoranda, or reports from 2000 to present referencing Ehlers–Danlos Syndrome (EDS) or related connective tissue disorders in the context of:
	•	Military, aerospace, or high-performance physical applications, including injury modeling, joint stability under extreme conditions, and unique proprioceptive profiles in EDS cohorts
	•	Performance prediction algorithms or training adaptations for individuals with hypermobility or connective tissue fragility
	•	DARPA or DoD-funded studies into connective tissue vulnerability or resilience under environmental stressors (e.g., extreme temperature, microgravity, high G-force)
	•	Research into environmental triggers or exacerbators of EDS symptoms, including chemical exposures, airborne particulates, mold, or electromagnetic/radiation environments
	•	Cross-analysis of EDS with mast cell activation syndrome (MCAS) or dysautonomia in environmental exposure contexts
	•	Interagency or contractor communications on predictive modeling or risk stratification of EDS patients in occupational or deployment settings

Keywords: Ehlers–Danlos Syndrome, hypermobility, connective tissue disorder, performance prediction, injury modeling, DARPA, DoD, environmental exposure, mast cell activation syndrome, dysautonomia, microgravity, proprioception

Request:

Requesting any and all research proposals, contracts, internal communications, reports, or contractor deliverables from 1990 to present referencing Ehlers–Danlos Syndrome (EDS), Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome (POTS), or related connective tissue and autonomic disorders in the context of:
	•	Simulated, altered, or variable gravity environments conducted on Earth (“terrestrial microgravity” or “artificial gravity” conditions)
	•	Studies involving the physiological or behavioral impact of small changes in gravitational load, acceleration, or gravitational gradient
	•	Medical or occupational health evaluations of individuals with EDS or POTS in centrifuge, parabolic flight, or gravity-simulation environments
	•	Research into circulatory, musculoskeletal, or neurological responses to gravitational variation in hypermobile or dysautonomia-affected populations
	•	Interagency or contractor collaborations on environmental exposure testing where gravitational conditions are a controlled or independent variable
	•	Any use of gravitational field manipulation technology for human performance modeling, risk assessment, or environmental safety evaluation
