To NIH / NIMH / NIDDK (National Institute of Mental Health & National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases, NIH)

Request:

Requesting any and all documents, research proposals, internal communications, or contractor reports referencing binge eating disorder or binge-type behavioral research from 1990 to present.

This includes:
	•	Studies on neurocognitive and neurobiological mechanisms of binge eating, with emphasis on impulse control, satiety cues, and reward pathways
	•	Research exploring the modulation of feeding behaviors through neuromodulation, brain–computer interface, or other neurotechnology systems
	•	Contractor or university projects funded under NIH or NIMH grants focused on binge eating disorder and neural circuit regulation
	•	Behavioral economics or consumer demand studies linked to binge eating or compulsive food intake
	•	Risk-benefit assessments of interventions aimed at enhancing, suppressing, or redirecting binge-type behaviors
	•	Interagency or contractor communications connecting binge eating research to marketing, product demand, or consumption behavior modulation

Keywords: binge eating disorder, binge-type behavior, impulse control, satiety cues, reward pathways, neuromodulation, neurotechnology, brain–computer interface, behavioral economics, consumer demand
