To: Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) / National Institutes of Health (NIH) / Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) / Food and Drug Administration (FDA)

Request:

Requesting any and all documents, clinical trial data, internal communications, or vendor contracts from 1950 to present relating to the prescription, evaluation, and efficacy of corrective shoes or orthopedic footwear in pediatric populations.

This includes:
	•	Clinical studies assessing corrective shoe use for flat feet, intoeing, or other gait abnormalities
	•	Internal memoranda or reviews questioning the medical necessity of corrective footwear
	•	Communications regarding public health guidelines or recommendations on pediatric orthopedic shoe prescriptions
	•	FDA or NIH documentation on device classification, regulation, or safety of corrective footwear
	•	Reports on long-term musculoskeletal, neurological, or developmental outcomes associated with childhood use of corrective shoes

Keywords: corrective shoes, orthopedic footwear, pediatric gait, flat feet, intoeing, outtoeing, musculoskeletal development, posture correction, gait analysis, clinical efficacy, device regulation
