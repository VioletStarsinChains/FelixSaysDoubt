To: U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) / Office of Research and Development
Cc: U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC); Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) / National Center for Environmental Health

Request:

Requesting any and all documents, studies, reports, internal communications, contracts, or risk assessments from January 1, 2005 to present concerning sand used in consumer or recreational settings as a potential vector for environmental contamination and adverse health outcomes.

This includes but is not limited to:
	•	Analyses of chemical contaminants (PFAS, heavy metals, microplastics, acrylamide, combustion byproducts) present in playground sand, sandboxes, or recreational beach sand
	•	Documentation on microbial or fungal colonization of sand, including tuberculosis or other persistent pathogens
	•	Studies on inhalation exposure, skin contact, or ingestion of contaminated sand by children and associated developmental or neurological risks
	•	Internal or external communications regarding regulatory standards for playground or landscaping sand
	•	Interagency reports or memoranda on the role of sand in spreading environmental toxins, biofilms, or particulate matter in child-centered environments
	•	Risk assessment and mitigation strategies developed by EPA, CPSC, or CDC regarding sand contamination in consumer markets or public spaces

Keywords: sand, playgrounds, sandboxes, children, environmental contamination, PFAS, heavy metals, microplastics, acrylamide, pathogens, EPA, CPSC, CDC
