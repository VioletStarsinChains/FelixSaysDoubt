To Department of Defense (DoD) / U.S. Air Force / U.S. Army Corps of Engineers

Request:

Requesting any and all documents, research proposals, internal communications, contracts, field reports, and environmental assessments from January 1, 1990 to present referencing the use, testing, storage, or experimental deployment of tactical herbicides (including but not limited to 2,4-D, 2,4,5-T, Agent Orange, Agent White, Agent Blue, picloram, imazapyr, glyphosate at tactical concentrations) in the continental United States for the purposes of vegetation management, defoliation, or fire control.

This includes:
	•	Records of aerial or ground spraying missions on or near military training ranges, buffer zones, or communities adjacent to installations
	•	Environmental impact assessments and risk analyses concerning increased wildfire susceptibility after vegetation die-off
	•	Interagency communications with USDA, USFS, EPA, or state authorities regarding tactical or experimental herbicide use for fire modeling, fuel load management, or vegetation clearing
	•	Storage, transport, or disposal logs of legacy defoliants at domestic bases (including Little Rock AFB and other herbicide transit/storage sites)
	•	Research proposals or DARPA/DoD-funded projects assessing the behavioral, ecological, or infrastructure impacts of wildfire scenarios created through chemical vegetation management

Keywords: Agent Orange, Agent White, Agent Blue, tactical herbicide, vegetation management, defoliation, wildfire susceptibility, fire modeling, Army Corps, DARPA, LRAFB

To Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) / I2O / TTO / DSO / STO

Request:

Requesting any and all research proposals, BAAs/solicitations, white papers, statements of work, contracts/task orders, test plans, after-action reports, modeling datasets/simulation code, internal communications, and interagency/vendor/academic correspondence from January 1, 1990 to present referencing the deliberate manipulation of vegetation (including herbicide/defoliant use) to engineer wildfire scenarios for the purposes of behavioral futures modeling, evacuation/compliance studies, infrastructure resilience testing, or market/insurance impact analysis.

This includes:
	•	Program briefs and funded project records describing “engineered wildfire,” “fuel load reduction via chemical means,” “defoliation for scenario generation,” or equivalent terminology
	•	Human-behavior modeling deliverables (agent-based models, synthetic population datasets, mobility/evacuation telemetry, information-operations overlays) derived from or calibrated to chemically induced vegetation die-off and subsequent fire behavior
	•	Interagency correspondence with DoD services, USFS/USDA, EPA/OPP, DHS, DOI, state forestry/fire agencies, or utilities regarding experimental vegetation management, experimental use permits (EUPs), or fire-scenario field trials
	•	Data acquisition plans and collected datasets (remote sensing, UAS/aircraft ISR, ground sensor networks) measuring vegetation mortality, flammability, ignition probability, and spread following herbicide/defoliant application
	•	Risk, ethics, and legal reviews addressing domestic use of herbicides/defoliants for test or modeling purposes, including wildland–urban interface (WUI) implications and community impact assessments
	•	Contractor and academic partner communications on chemical classes and concentrations (e.g., 2,4-D, 2,4,5-T, picloram, imazapyr, glyphosate; legacy “tactical herbicides” such as Agent Orange/White/Blue) used as variables in wildfire scenario design

Keywords: DARPA, engineered wildfire, defoliant, herbicide fuel treatment, behavioral futures, evacuation modeling, agent-based models, WUI, interagency correspondence, experimental use permit (EUP), remote sensing, resilience testing
