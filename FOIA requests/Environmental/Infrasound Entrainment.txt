To:
• Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA)
• National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health (NIOSH)
• Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) / Office of Research and Development
• Department of Labor (DOL)

Request:

Requesting any and all reports, studies, complaints, or interagency communications from January 1, 2000 to present concerning infrasound, low-frequency sound, or vibration-based entrainment methods in factory, warehouse, or industrial manufacturing settings.

This includes:
	•	Occupational health and safety assessments documenting physiological or behavioral effects of infrasound or low-frequency exposure in workers
	•	Research proposals or internal studies on entrainment effects of low-frequency sound in controlled industrial environments
	•	Records of worker complaints or union grievances citing incapacitation, disorientation, or stress symptoms associated with infrasound in factories or warehouses
	•	Enforcement actions, inspections, or hazard citations related to sound/vibration exposure in industrial facilities
	•	Communications between OSHA, NIOSH, EPA, and private sector partners regarding the deployment of infrasound or vibration-modulating infrastructure in factory settings
	•	Interagency analyses of potential compliance, productivity, or behavioral impacts of low-frequency sound exposure in occupational environments

Keywords: infrasound hazard, low-frequency sound exposure, factory entrainment, worker compliance testing, occupational acoustics, vibration stress, NIOSH studies, OSHA enforcement, industrial noise modeling, EPA research
