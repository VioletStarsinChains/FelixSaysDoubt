To Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) / Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) / Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC)

Request:

Requesting any and all records, laboratory analyses, supplier certifications, interagency communications, or oversight reports from 1990 to present concerning the use of organic-derived materials, cremated remains, bone ash, human hair, or other human/animal remains in the production, formulation, or application of powder coatings used in consumer products, industrial equipment, furniture, vehicles, or appliances.

This includes:
	•	Records describing or authorizing the substitution of cremains, bone ash, or organic mineral powders for conventional fillers or pigments (e.g., calcium carbonate, talc, titanium dioxide) in powder coating blends.
	•	Laboratory analyses or supplier certifications identifying organic-derived materials in powder coatings applied to consumer products or industrial equipment.
	•	OSHA or EPA risk assessments regarding worker exposure during the handling, spraying, or curing of powder coatings containing cremains or bone ash.
	•	CPSC records of consumer safety complaints, hazard reports, or recalls tied to products coated with powders containing organic remains.
	•	Communications between EPA, OSHA, CPSC, or contractors regarding the environmental, health, or ethical implications of incorporating cremains or organic-derived fillers into powder coatings.

Keywords: powder coating, cremains, cremated remains, bone ash, keratin, organic filler, calcium phosphate, calcium carbonate substitute, recycled organics, OSHA, EPA, CPSC
