To EPA / CDC / OSHA

Request:

Requesting any and all records, internal communications, contracts, or research proposals regarding the incorporation of cremated human remains (cremains) into construction or industrial materials, from 2000 to present.

This includes:
	•	Research on the use of cremains as a supplementary material in concrete, cement, ceramics, or glass
	•	Risk assessments concerning toxic metals or chemical residues in cremains (e.g., mercury, chemotherapy byproducts, medical implants) entering industrial material streams
	•	Oversight or contractor reports on public health or environmental impacts of cremains used in large-scale infrastructure or consumer products
	•	Interagency communications on cremains as an alternative waste management pathway intersecting with construction, recycling, or urban development sectors
	•	Audits, pilot projects, or regulatory reviews of vendors marketing cremains-derived construction materials

Keywords: cremains, cremated remains, human ashes, concrete, cement, ceramic, glass, construction material, toxic metals, waste management, environmental health
