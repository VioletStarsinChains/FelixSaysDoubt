To Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) / Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) / Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD)

Request:

Requesting any and all records, laboratory analyses, interagency communications, contractor reports, or oversight documents regarding the use of organic-derived materials, cremated remains, bone ash, or other human/animal remains in the manufacture of interior and exterior architectural paints and coatings, from 1990 to present.

This includes:
	•	Safety testing or compositional analyses of paints that identified the use of bone ash, cremains, or human/animal remains as fillers, pigments, or extenders.
	•	Import inspection reports or EPA/CPSC records concerning architectural paints marketed as “recycled,” “eco-friendly,” or “sustainable” that incorporated organic-derived material.
	•	HUD housing safety audits or communications related to the presence of organic remains in wall paints, stuccos, or exterior coatings used in federally funded housing programs.
	•	Contractor or supplier communications with EPA, CPSC, or HUD about the substitution of organic or cremated material for conventional paint fillers (chalk, talc, silica, titanium dioxide).
	•	Records of adverse health complaints, product recalls, or consumer safety incidents tied to paints later found to contain organic-derived or cremated remains.

Keywords: architectural paint, exterior coating, recycled paint, eco paint, cremains, cremated remains, bone ash, organic filler, pigment extender, CPSC, HUD, EPA oversight
