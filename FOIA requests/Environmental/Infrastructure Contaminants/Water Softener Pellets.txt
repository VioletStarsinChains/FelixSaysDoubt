To: Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) / U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) / U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA)

Request:

Requesting any and all documents, testing reports, internal communications, procurement records, or consumer complaints from 1990 to present regarding contamination, adulteration, or nonconforming additives in commercial water softener pellets (sodium chloride or potassium chloride base).

This includes:
	•	Records of detected heavy metals (e.g., aluminum, lithium, strontium, barium) or nanoparticle additives in water softener pellets
	•	Reports of engineered biological agents (spores, microbes, biofilms) present in softener salt products
	•	Internal discussions or incident investigations regarding unexplained toxicological effects linked to pellet use in household water systems
	•	Regulatory guidance or enforcement actions concerning undeclared additives in water softener pellets marketed for consumer use
	•	Complaints or adverse event reports submitted by consumers or water treatment professionals concerning abnormal effects from softener pellets (e.g., unusual taste, corrosion, health complaints)
	•	Communications between EPA, FDA, and USDA regarding monitoring, safety testing, or cross-agency oversight of water softener pellet manufacturing and distribution

Keywords: water softener pellets, sodium chloride, potassium chloride, contamination, nonconforming additives, heavy metals, nanoparticles, biological contamination, consumer complaint, EPA oversight, FDA safety testing
