FOIA – Titanium Supply Chain Risks Post-2022

To: Department of Energy (DOE – Critical Materials Institute) / Department of Defense (DoD – Defense Logistics Agency, Office of Industrial Policy) / U.S. Geological Survey (USGS – National Minerals Information Center)

Request:
Requesting any and all records, contracts, reports, or interagency communications from 2015 to present concerning U.S. assessments of titanium supply chain risks following the outbreak of war in Ukraine in 2022.

This includes:
	•	Reports on the availability, disruption, or rerouting of titanium ore or sponge imports from Russia and Ukraine
	•	Communications regarding sanctions, export restrictions, or alternative sourcing of titanium for aerospace, defense, or medical applications
	•	Contracts or procurement records reflecting emergency stockpiling of titanium or titanium alloys
	•	Analyses of titanium supply risks in relation to continuity of aircraft, drones, or medical device production

Keywords: titanium supply chain, Ukraine conflict, Russia sanctions, titanium sponge, aerospace materials, defense logistics, DOE, DLA, USGS
