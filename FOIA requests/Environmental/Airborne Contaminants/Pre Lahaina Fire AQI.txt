To Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) / Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) / Hawaii Department of Health (DOH) / Hawaii Emergency Management Agency (HI-EMA)

Request:

Requesting any and all documents, monitoring data, inspection reports, audits, or internal communications concerning environmental contaminants in Lahaina, Maui, in the period leading up to the August 8, 2023 wildfire.

This includes:
	•	Air quality monitoring data (volatile organic compounds, amines, particulates, combustion byproducts, industrial emissions) collected between 2015–2023 in Lahaina or surrounding areas
	•	Soil and water sampling results from Lahaina prior to August 2023, including testing for decomposition-associated compounds (indole, skatole, putrescine, cadaverine), oxidizers, or petroleum-related contaminants
	•	Public health surveillance data or environmental health complaints from residents reporting unusual odors, respiratory issues, or unexplained illnesses in Lahaina before the fire
	•	Internal agency communications or reports discussing environmental stressors or contaminants in Maui and their potential health impacts on residents prior to August 2023
	•	FEMA or HI-EMA risk assessments addressing chemical hazards or environmental vulnerabilities in Lahaina prior to the wildfire
	•	Any contracts or collaborations with third-party monitoring agencies conducting environmental testing in Lahaina between 2015–2023

Keywords: Lahaina environmental monitoring, Maui contaminants, air quality, soil sampling, volatile amines, decomposition volatiles, petroleum contaminants, EPA monitoring, FEMA risk assessment, Hawaii DOH
