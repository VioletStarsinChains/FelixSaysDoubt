FOIA — Taggants in Chalk (Sidewalk and Classroom Use)

To Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) / Environmental Protection Agency (EPA, Office of Chemical Safety and Pollution Prevention) / Food and Drug Administration (Center for Food Safety and Applied Nutrition, Office of Cosmetics and Colors) / Federal Bureau of Investigation (Laboratory Division; Operational Technology Division) / Department of Homeland Security (Science & Technology Directorate)

Request:

Requesting any and all contracts, regulatory filings, laboratory analyses, toxicological assessments, research proposals, consumer complaints, enforcement actions, forensic validation studies, interagency memoranda, and internal communications from 1990 to present regarding the inclusion of taggants, tracers, or covert identifiers in chalk products marketed for sidewalk, classroom, or consumer use.

This includes:
	•	Records of nanoparticle, isotopic, fluorescent, or polymer additives used in chalk formulations for purposes of traceability, forensic analysis, or surveillance telemetry
	•	CPSC, EPA, or FDA toxicological reviews on the safety and health implications of taggant additives in chalk marketed to children or educational institutions
	•	FBI Laboratory protocols on the use of tagged chalk residues as forensic evidence in investigations (e.g., trace identifiers, fluorescent or isotopic markers)
	•	DHS or interagency communications on the use of sidewalk or classroom chalk as covert carriers of taggants for population monitoring, compliance testing, or behavioral modeling
	•	Vendor contracts, cooperative agreements, or SBIR/STTR grants with chalk manufacturers or pigment suppliers producing tagged formulations
	•	Reports of adverse events, consumer complaints, or occupational safety concerns related to chalk products containing taggant additives

Keywords: sidewalk chalk taggants, classroom chalk tracers, nanoparticle pigments, isotopic markers, forensic chalk, CPSC consumer safety, EPA toxicology, FDA cosmetics, FBI Laboratory trace evidence, DHS surveillance telemetry
