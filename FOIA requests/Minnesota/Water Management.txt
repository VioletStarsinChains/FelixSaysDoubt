Data Practices Act Request: Smart Irrigation Systems – Minneapolis

To: City of Minneapolis – Public Works Department / Minneapolis Park & Recreation Board / Minneapolis Division of Sustainability

Request:
Requesting any and all contracts, purchase orders, grants, reports, pilot program documentation, or interdepartmental communications from 2000 to present concerning the development, procurement, or deployment of smart irrigation systems or aerosolized water recycling technologies within the City of Minneapolis.

This includes:
	•	Contracts with private vendors or contractors providing “smart sprinklers,” mist irrigation, or sensor-enabled irrigation systems for public parks, boulevards, or residential demonstration projects
	•	Records of pilot programs involving water-saving or aerosolized sprinkler technology in Minneapolis neighborhoods or public spaces
	•	Reports on environmental impacts, efficiency outcomes, or public health assessments of aerosolized irrigation projects
	•	Communications with state or federal agencies regarding funding or technical assistance for smart irrigation in Minneapolis

Keywords: smart irrigation, aerosolized sprinklers, water recycling, mist irrigation, fog irrigation, Minneapolis Public Works, Park Board

Data Practices Act Request: Sonic / Ultrasonic Shower and Cleaning Technologies – Minneapolis & Minnesota

To: City of Minneapolis – Public Works Department / Minneapolis Division of Sustainability / Minnesota Department of Health (MDH) / Minnesota Pollution Control Agency (MPCA)

Request:
Requesting any and all contracts, grants, reports, pilot program documentation, or interdepartmental communications from 2000 to present concerning the development, procurement, or testing of sonic or ultrasonic cleaning and shower technologies as alternatives to conventional water-based hygiene systems.

This includes:
	•	Records of pilot projects involving ultrasonic showers, mist-based hygiene systems, or low-water/no-water cleaning demonstrations in municipal or state facilities
	•	Contracts with vendors or research institutions providing vibration-based or air-jet hygiene technologies
	•	Reports on water conservation outcomes or environmental assessments related to sonic/ultrasonic shower systems
	•	Communications with federal agencies regarding funding, grants, or technical support for non-traditional hygiene technologies tested in Minnesota
	•	Records of public health or safety reviews concerning the use of sonic hygiene devices in public, residential, or healthcare settings

Keywords: sonic shower, ultrasonic cleaning, water conservation, hygiene pilot, Minneapolis Public Works, MDH, MPCA
