To FBI (Laboratory Division; Operational Technology Division; Behavioral Analysis Unit) / U.S. Department of Justice (National Institute of Justice; Civil Rights Division) / Food and Drug Administration (Center for Food Safety and Applied Nutrition, Office of Cosmetics and Colors) / Department of Homeland Security (Science & Technology Directorate)

Request:

Requesting any and all documents, contracts, laboratory methods, research proposals, forensic validation studies, internal communications, consumer complaints, or interagency memoranda concerning the inclusion of taggants, covert identifiers, or trace materials in hair dye products formulated in unnatural or non-biological colors (e.g., blue, green, purple, neon, metallic, or UV-reactive shades) from 1995 to present.

This includes:
	•	FBI and NIJ protocols for detection, classification, or forensic use of tagged hair dyes as markers of identity, affiliation, or presence at a location
	•	Records of nanoparticle, isotopic, fluorescent, or polymer additives embedded in bright or non-natural hair dyes for purposes of surveillance, dissident monitoring, or behavioral telemetry
	•	FDA and CFSAN evaluations of cosmetic safety, toxicology, and disclosure regarding non-declared taggant additives in hair colorants
	•	DHS S&T or DOJ Civil Rights Division communications on the equity, civil liberties, or targeting implications of disproportionately monitoring populations using non-standard hair colors
	•	Vendor contracts, cooperative agreements, or SBIR/STTR grants with dye manufacturers or chemical suppliers producing tagged formulations for controlled distribution
	•	Interagency discussions on using dyed hair as a voluntary or involuntary biometric marker, including compliance testing, protest surveillance, or dissident tracking

Keywords: hair dye taggants, unnatural colorants, dissident surveillance, fluorescent pigments, nanoparticle additives, forensic trace evidence, behavioral telemetry, FDA cosmetics, NIJ research, Civil Rights Division
