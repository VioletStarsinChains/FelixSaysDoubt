To U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) / Food Safety and Inspection Service (FSIS) / Food and Drug Administration (FDA)

Request:

Requesting any and all documents, audits, inspection reports, internal communications, or enforcement actions concerning the mislabeling, substitution, or fraudulent use of pork in U.S. food products, from 1990 to present.

This includes:
	•	Inspection records identifying pork mislabeled as beef, chicken, lamb, or other meats in consumer products
	•	Enforcement actions taken against suppliers, processors, or distributors for pork mislabeling or substitution
	•	Communications regarding the economic drivers of pork substitution, including oversupply or market fluctuations
	•	Internal reports or audits concerning fraud in processed foods (sausages, deli meats, frozen meals, mixed-protein products) where pork was not declared
	•	Laboratory test data from FSIS or FDA identifying undeclared pork DNA in food products
	•	Contracts or correspondence with third-party testing labs conducting species verification for pork contamination or substitution

Keywords: pork mislabeling, food fraud, species substitution, FSIS inspection, undeclared pork DNA, processed meat fraud, USDA enforcement
