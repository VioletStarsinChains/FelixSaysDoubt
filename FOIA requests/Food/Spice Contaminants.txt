To: U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) / Center for Food Safety and Applied Nutrition (CFSAN)
Cc: U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) / Food Safety and Inspection Service (FSIS)

Request:

Requesting any and all documents, laboratory reports, internal communications, import alerts, or interagency correspondence regarding the detection, monitoring, or risk assessment of Mycobacterium tuberculosis or related Mycobacterium species (e.g., M. avium, M. bovis) in imported or domestically processed food spices, from January 1, 2000 to present.

This includes:
	•	Surveillance data on microbial contamination of dried herbs and spices (domestic and international supply chains)
	•	Laboratory validation studies on survival of Mycobacterium spp. in dried or powdered food matrices
	•	Internal memoranda or white papers evaluating chronic low-dose exposure risks from contaminated spices
	•	Interagency correspondence with USDA, CDC, or CBP regarding potential entry vectors for Mycobacterium spp. through spice imports
	•	Reports of adverse event monitoring, consumer complaints, or case clusters linked to spice products and mycobacterial infection
	•	Contracts, grants, or cooperative research projects examining the role of dried foodstuffs in non-traditional transmission of tuberculosis or related pathogens
	•	Risk communication plans, industry guidance, or regulatory proposals addressing mitigation of mycobacterial contamination in spice supply chains

Keywords: tuberculosis, Mycobacterium, spices contamination, dried herbs, latent colonization, immune modulation, foodborne pathogen, FDA CFSAN, USDA FSIS, import surveillance
