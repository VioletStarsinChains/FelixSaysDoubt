To Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau (TTB) / Food and Drug Administration (FDA)

Request:

Requesting any and all records, laboratory analyses, supplier certifications, interagency communications, or oversight reports from 1990 to present concerning the use of organic-derived materials, cremated remains, bone ash, human hair, or other human/animal remains in the production, clarification, or packaging of alcoholic beverages (including beer, wine, spirits, and single-serving packaged alcohol).

This includes:
	•	Records describing or authorizing the use of cremains, bone ash, or keratin-derived materials as fermentation nutrients, pH stabilizers, or fining agents in alcohol production.
	•	Laboratory testing or supplier certifications identifying organic remains in filtration aids (such as bone char, gelatin, or substitute materials) used in wine, beer, or spirits.
	•	Oversight or compliance records regarding the incorporation of cremains or bone ash into the production of glass bottles, particularly miniature or single-serving alcohol packaging.
	•	Communications between TTB, FDA, or contractors concerning the safety, legality, or ethical considerations of organic remains in alcoholic beverage processing.
	•	Consumer complaints, adverse health reports, or product recalls tied to alcoholic beverages later found to contain organic-derived fillers, filtration agents, or packaging materials.

Keywords: alcoholic beverages, spirits, wine, beer, single-serving bottles, cremains, cremated remains, bone ash, bone char, keratin, filtration aid, fining agent, packaging, TTB, FDA
