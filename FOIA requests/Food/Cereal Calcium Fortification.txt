To Food and Drug Administration (FDA) / U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA)

Request:

Requesting any and all records, research proposals, contracts, vendor deliverables, interagency communications, or oversight reports from 1990 to present concerning the use of nontraditional or organic-derived compounds in the fortification of cereal and grain-based products.

This includes:
	•	Records describing the use of calcium phosphate, protein powders, or mineral blends derived from nontraditional sources, including organic remains or cremated materials, in cereal fortification.
	•	FDA or USDA evaluations of supplier sourcing, safety profiles, or substitution practices for calcium or mineral additives in cereals.
	•	Laboratory analyses or environmental monitoring reports regarding the presence of nonstandard organic particulates in fortified cereals marketed in the U.S.
	•	Communications between FDA, USDA, or affiliated contractors regarding the sourcing and testing of calcium phosphate or related mineral additives in cereals.
	•	Oversight reviews or risk assessments addressing the ethical, health, or labeling implications of using organic-derived fortification materials in consumer grain products.

Keywords: cereal fortification, calcium phosphate, mineral additives, protein fortification, cremated remains, organic particulates, FDA, USDA
